Using the powers package

Sarafa Iyaniwura

2018-11-14

Note: This package isn’t actually meant to be “serious”. I made it just for teaching purposes.

Want to compute powers without having to use ^ or **? Give sqrt some friends by loading the powers package!

library(powers)

Want squares, cubes, or reciprocals? Easy.

square(1:10)
##  [1]   1   4   9  16  25  36  49  64  81 100
cube(1:10)
##  [1]    1    8   27   64  125  216  343  512  729 1000
reciprocal(1:10)
##  [1] 1.0000000 0.5000000 0.3333333 0.2500000 0.2000000 0.1666667 0.1428571
##  [8] 0.1250000 0.1111111 0.1000000

We can also compute Box-Cox transformation of a vector.

vec <- 1:10
L <- 4
Box_Cox(vec,L)
##  [1]    0.00    3.75   20.00   63.75  156.00  323.75  600.00 1023.75
##  [9] 1640.00 2499.75

apply-family friendly, too!

my_list <- list(1:10, 0.5, -0.7)
## So base-R-boring!
lapply(my_list, function(x) x^2)
## [[1]]
##  [1]   1   4   9  16  25  36  49  64  81 100
## 
## [[2]]
## [1] 0.25
## 
## [[3]]
## [1] 0.49
## Use powers instead!
lapply(my_list, square)
## [[1]]
##  [1]   1   4   9  16  25  36  49  64  81 100
## 
## [[2]]
## [1] 0.25
## 
## [[3]]
## [1] 0.49

It even comes with the ability to display its output in plot form!

reciprocal(1:10, plot_it=TRUE)

##  [1] 1.0000000 0.5000000 0.3333333 0.2500000 0.2000000 0.1666667 0.1428571
##  [8] 0.1250000 0.1111111 0.1000000
Box_Cox(vec,L, plot_it=TRUE)

##  [1]    0.00    3.75   20.00   63.75  156.00  323.75  600.00 1023.75
##  [9] 1640.00 2499.75

Wow!!!
